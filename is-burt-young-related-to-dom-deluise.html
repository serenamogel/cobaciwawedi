Is Burt Young Related to Dom Deluise?

Burt Young, whose real name was Gerald Tommaso DeLouis, was an American painter, author, and actor. In the Rocky film series, he portrayed Rocky Balboa’s brother-in-law and closest friend, Paulie Pennino. For his role in the first film, he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.

Who Is Burt Young?

Young gained notoriety for portraying gritty working-class Italian-American characters. His most well-known performance was in Rocky (1976), when he played Paulie, the buddy and eventual brother-in-law of Rocky Balboa. For this portrayal, Young was nominated for an Oscar in the Best Supporting Actor category.

Along with Sylvester Stallone, Stu Nahan, and Tony Burton, he is one of four actors who have starred in each of the first six Rocky movies (though Talia Shire, who has been in the first five, makes a flashback appearance in the sixth).

In the 2015 movie Creed, Young did not play the same character again; the character was said to have passed away in 2012.

Chinatown, Convoy, Back to School, The Pope of Greenwich Village, Once Upon a Time in America, Last Exit to Brooklyn, Downtown: A Street Tale, and Amityville II: The Possession are just a few of the movies in which Young starred.

Young appeared in The Rockford Files, Baretta, Law & Order, Walker, Texas Ranger, All In The Family, M*A*S*H, and Miami Vice, among other television shows.

He appeared on The Sopranos (“Another Toothpick”) as the father of Bobby Baccalieri, who is ill with cancer and comes out of retirement to kill his godson, “Mustang Sally” Intile, for beating a family friend violently just for talking to Intile’s girlfriend.

He played a retired drill instructor in the short-lived 1987 ABC sitcom Roomies, a reference to his time spent in the United States Marine Corps, where his character chooses to attend college after retirement.

2017 saw Burt Young make a comeback to the stage in Dave Varriale’s play The Last Vig, where he played an elderly crime boss. The performance took place at Los Angeles’ The Zephyr Theatre from January 14 to February 19, 2017.

Burt Young Wife

Gloria, Young’s wife, passed away in 1974. He had a grandson and a daughter. He was a resident of New York’s Port Washington.

Young was the owner of a restaurant in New York’s Bronx. He ran in the New York City Marathon in 1984. On October 8, 2023, Burt Young passed away in Los Angeles at the age of 83.

Adrienne Shelly Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Adrienne Levine (June 24, 1966 – November 1, 2006), best known by her stage as Adrienne Shelly (sometimes attributed as Adrienne Shelley), was an American actress, director, and playwright who worked mostly in the entertainment industry. Shelly made her reputation in indie films such as 1989’s The Unbelievable Truth and 1990’s Trust before pursuing a career in writing and directing. She wrote, co-starred in, and directed the 2007 picture Waitress, which was nominated for five Oscars and had many film festival nominations. Shelly was discovered deceased at her work studio in Greenwich Village on November 1, 2006, according to police. Although Shelly’s death was originally ruled a suicide, authorities subsequently apprehended a construction worker who confessed to killing her after she caught him looting her apartment and calling the cops on him. She and her husband founded the Adrienne Shelly Foundation, which provides artists with scholarships, production grants, finishing money, and living stipends, among other things. The Adrienne Shelly Award, named in her honour by the Women Film Critics Circle, is given annually to the film that the group believes “most passionately condemns violence against women.”

In Memoriam: Dusty Street – A Legendary Radio Host

The world of radio and music is in mourning following the passing of Dusty Street, a legendary radio host who left us on October 21, 2023, at the age of 77. Dusty was a trailblazer in her field, known for being one of the first female rock DJs on the West Coast. Her incredible career included stints at iconic stations such as KROQ, KLOS, and SiriusXM. She was celebrated for her bold personality, her deep passion for music, and her close friendships with numerous rock stars. She departed after a long and courageous battle with cancer, surrounded by her loved ones.

Dusty Street’s journey was a remarkable one, and here is a brief biography that honors her life:

Born as Dusty Frances Street on March 11, 1946, in San Francisco, California, Dusty’s early years were colored by the vibrant Haight-Ashbury district, where she was immersed in the counterculture scene of the 1960s. Her circle of friends included luminaries like Janis Joplin, Grace Slick, and other influential musicians.

In 1967, Dusty embarked on her radio career at KMPX, a pioneering underground radio station. She later found her home at KSAN, where she became an iconic voice of the free-form format.

In 1978, Dusty made her mark in Los Angeles when she joined KROQ, a station that was instrumental in promoting punk and new wave music. Her departure from KROQ in 1989, due to differences with the station’s management regarding its commercial direction, marked a turning point in her career.

Throughout her career, Dusty made significant contributions to various radio stations in Los Angeles, including KLOS and KWST. In 1996, she relocated to Cleveland to take on a role as a curator and DJ at the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame, further solidifying her place in the world of music history.

In 2004, Dusty Street joined SiriusXM, where she hosted “Dusty Street’s Classic Vinyl.” Later, she graced the airwaves of SiriusXM Deep Tracks, delighting listeners with classic rock tunes and sharing stories from her illustrious career.

Dusty was married twice during her lifetime and, while she did not have children, she is survived by her longtime partner, Scott Primeau, and her brother, David Street.

Dusty Street’s legacy lives on in the hearts of those who were fortunate enough to experience her influence in the world of music and radio. Her memory will forever be intertwined with the music she cherished and shared with the world. May she rest in peace.

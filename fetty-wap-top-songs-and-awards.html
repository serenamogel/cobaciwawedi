Fetty Wap top songs and awards

American rapper, singer, and songwriter Willie Maxwell II, better known by his stage name Fetty Wap, is a rapper.

He was able to manage 300 gigs thanks to the popularity of Trap Queen, his most well-known song, which peaked at number two on the US Billboard Hot 2015 chart.

Fetty Wap was able to establish his reputation as a talented rapper as he rose in fame and popularity. On June 7, 1991, Wap was born in Paterson, New Jersey, in the United States. Fetty Wap is an American citizen of African-American origin.

He was born with glaucoma in both of his eyes, thus doctors were unable to salvage his left one. His mother and father’s names are kept a secret, and he has a sister by the name of Divinity Maxwell.

Fetty Wap started singing at an early age, and in 2013 he thought about making singing his career. Fetty Wap’s first song to be released commercially, “Trap Queen was made accessible at the start of 2014.

After a few months, the song became platinum and today has over 130 million plays on SoundCloud and 525 million views on YouTube. Initially, the song didn’t get much attention or views. After Va, the success of “For Trap Queen, he inked a recording deal with 300 Entertainments.

Fetty Wap top songs

My Way, $ave Dat Money, All In My Head, RGF Island, Promise, Wake Up, and Jimmy Choo are a few of Fetty Wap’s top tracks.
